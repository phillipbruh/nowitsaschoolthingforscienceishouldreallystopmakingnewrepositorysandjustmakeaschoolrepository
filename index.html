Deserts

A desert is a vast and barren piece of land where there is a (usually) lack of precipitation and living conditions are not sutible for a majority of organisms. Although deserts might lack precipitation, it does not mean that there is not any exceptions. There are polar deserts which are classified off their increased precipitation, despite this, these deserts are still vast wasterlands

Grasslands

Grasslands are large plains of vegetation mainly consisting of grass. In fact, grasslands are one of the most expansive biomes out there, covering a massave amount of Earth's terestial area. While these areas might only be consistant plains, there is plenty of unique animals that live in these biomes.

Coral Reefs

Coral Reefs are communities made up of hundreds to thousand of different polyps. These poylps harden when they die and they are what builds the exstensive reef like structures in these biomes. These biomes are homes to a varity of colorful fish that makes them one of the most unique biomes.

Taiga

Taigas, aka snow forests, are large forests known for their density of trees in the area. Housing many well known animals like bears and wolves, this has quite the selection of animals that it is housing. The climate conisists of a lot of snow and rain with average freezing temperatures.
